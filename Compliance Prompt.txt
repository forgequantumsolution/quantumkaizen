You are a regulatory compliance software architect specializing in Quality Management Systems (QMS), Document Management Systems (DMS), and Learning Management Systems (LMS). Your task is to perform a comprehensive update of all three systems to achieve and maintain full regulatory compliance across Pharmaceutical & Life Sciences, Chemical Manufacturing, Food & Beverage Production, and Automotive & Industrial Machinery Manufacturing.

The update must treat QMS, DMS, and LMS as an integrated compliance ecosystem:
  - QMS drives CAPA, change control, and risk events that trigger DMS document revisions and LMS retraining
  - DMS controls the lifecycle of all regulated documents referenced by QMS workflows and LMS curricula
  - LMS certifies personnel competency required before QMS process execution and DMS document effectuation

All three systems must share a unified audit trail, electronic signature framework compliant with 21 CFR Part 11 and EU GMP Annex 11, and ALCOA+ data integrity controls (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available).

  - Disaster recovery: version-integrity-verified backup; RTO/RPO per regulatory criticality tier

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

REGULATORY OBLIGATIONS:
  - IATF 16949:2016 Clause 7.2: determine competence of persons affecting conformity; provide training; evaluate effectiveness; retain documented evidence
  - IATF 16949 Clause 7.3: all employees aware of quality policy, quality objectives, and nonconformity consequences
  - EU Machinery Regulation (EU) 2023/1230 Article 10 / Annex I: operating instructions must cover personnel training requirements for safe use and maintenance

MANDATORY CURRICULA:
  - IATF 16949 / ISO 9001 QMS Awareness (all employees): quality policy, customer focus, process approach, risk thinking
  - APQP/PPAP (product/process engineers): phase gate deliverables, PSW sign-off authority, PPAP submission level determination
  - AIAG-VDA FMEA (FMEA team facilitators): 7-step methodology, Action Priority (AP) table, team facilitation technique, FMEA linkage to control plan
  - SPC (production engineers, operators): control chart types (Xbar-R, IMR, P, U), Cpk/Ppk interpretation, out-of-control rules (Nelson/WECO), reaction plan execution
  - MSA (metrology/QC personnel): gauge R&R (crossed and nested), attribute agreement analysis, calibration system linkage
  - 8D Problem Solving (quality engineers, team leaders): discipline-by-discipline with live case study; D3 containment authority, D6 permanent corrective action verification
  - ISO 26262 Functional Safety (safety engineers): ASIL determination, safety lifecycle phases, work product authorship, confirmation measure obligations per ISO 26262-2 Annex A
  - Machinery Safety (design engineers): EN ISO 12100 risk assessment methodology, PL/Category determination (ISO 13849-1), CE/UKCA technical file compilation, EU Machinery Regulation 2023/1230 transition requirements
  - REACH/RoHS/ELV Substance Compliance (materials/procurement): substance identification, supplier declaration process, IMDS submission workflow
  - LOTO / Machine Guarding / Confined Space (maintenance technicians): 29 CFR 1910.147 LOTO, 29 CFR 1910.212 machine guarding, 29 CFR 1910.146 confined space entry

COMPETENCY VALIDATION:
  - Core tool proficiency: practical FMEA facilitation, SPC chart analysis, and PPAP package review assessed by qualified assessors
  - ISO 26262 competence: role-based evidence per ISO 26262-2 Annex A Tables A.1 and A.2
  - IATF internal auditor: minimum 16-hour certified course; VDA 6.3 qualification for process auditors

RETENTION: minimum 15 years for safety/liability-affecting roles per IATF 16949 Clause 7.5.3.2; configure extended retention per OEM CSR

════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 4 — CROSS-SYSTEM INTEGRATION REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

QMS → DMS INTEGRATION:
  - CAPA actions and change control auto-route affected document owners for revision in DMS
  - Completed document revisions close corresponding QMS change control and CAPA action items
  - Nonconformance records automatically attach to relevant DMS specification or SOP for reference

QMS → LMS INTEGRATION:
  - Change control effectuation and CAPA closure trigger auto-assignment of role-specific retraining in LMS
  - Training completion evidence is a prerequisite gate for QMS process execution sign-off
  - CAPA root cause analysis identifying competency gaps auto-generates targeted LMS training assignment

DMS → LMS INTEGRATION:
  - New or revised documents auto-assign Read-and-Understand (R&U) acknowledgment with assessment to all affected roles in LMS
  - Document effectuation is blocked until LMS confirms minimum required role completion percentage
  - SOP version in use at time of training is version-locked in training record for traceability

UNIFIED COMPLIANCE INFRASTRUCTURE:
  - Single sign-on (SSO) and Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) across QMS/DMS/LMS per ISO 27001 least-privilege principle
  - Shared regulatory change monitoring engine: automated alerts on Federal Register, EUR-Lex, and industry body publications; impact assessment routed to QMS (process), DMS (document), and LMS (training) owners simultaneously
  - Unified compliance dashboard: open CAPA aging, document periodic review status, training compliance rate, audit finding trends — all by site, department, regulation, and inspection readiness score
  - Consolidated audit trail repository: single searchable log across all three systems per 21 CFR Part 11 / EU GMP Annex 11 requirements
  - Regulation version registry: effective dates, transition timelines, and impact assessments with tri-system action plans

PART 5 — VALIDATION & DATA INTEGRITY
════════════════════════════════════════════════

  - Validate QMS, DMS, and LMS as GAMP 5 (2nd ed.) Category 4/5 software with full IQ/OQ/PQ lifecycle, URS, Functional Specification (FS), and risk assessments
  - Deliver Validation Master Plan (VMP) covering all three systems with site-specific configuration rationale
  - ALCOA+ data integrity controls enforced at system, database, and user interface level across all modules

  - Periodic review of validated state: annual system review with change control-triggered re-validation assessment
  - User access review: quarterly RBAC access certification; immediate deactivation workflow on personnel departure
